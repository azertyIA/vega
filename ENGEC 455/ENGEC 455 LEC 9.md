Complex frequency represents exponential growth or decay. This can apply to both time and space. In a microwave, the waves are attenuated to the absorption of your food. Given:
> $e^{j(\omega t-kz)}=e^{j(\omega_Rt-kz)}e^{-\omega_It}$
> where $\omega=\omega_R+j\omega_I$

Given Generalized Ampere's Law:
> $\nabla\times\vec H=\vec J+\partial_t\vec D$
> $\nabla\times\vec H=\vec J+(j\epsilon_0\omega)\vec E$
> $\nabla\times\vec H=\sigma\vec E+(j\epsilon_0\omega)\vec E$
> $\nabla\times\vec H=j\epsilon_0\omega[1+\frac\sigma{j\omega\epsilon_0}]\vec E$
> $\nabla\times\vec H=j\omega\epsilon_{eff}\vec E$, $\epsilon_{eff}=\epsilon_0[1+\frac\sigma{j\omega\epsilon_0}]$

If this effective permittivity is much greater than 1, then it's a good conductor. If it's much less than 1, then it's a good insulator. 

How can you split between real components and imaginary components?
> $\frac\omega k=1/\sqrt{\epsilon_{eff}\mu_0}=\frac1{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0(1+\sigma/j\omega\epsilon_0)}}$
> $k=\omega{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0(1+\sigma/j\omega\epsilon_0)}}$
